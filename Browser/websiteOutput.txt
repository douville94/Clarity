<p><b><a href="/wiki/Urse_d%27Abetot" title="Urse d'Abetot">Urse d'Abetot</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Circa" title="Circa">c.</a> 1040&#160;– 1108) was a <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Worcestershire" title="High Sheriff of Worcestershire">Sheriff of Worcestershire</a> and royal official under Kings <a href="/wiki/William_I_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="William I of England">William&#160;I</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_II_of_England" title="William II of England">William II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henry_I_of_England" title="Henry I of England">Henry I</a>. Urse's lord in <a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a> was present at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hastings" title="Battle of Hastings">Battle of Hastings</a>, and Urse moved to England shortly after the <a href="/wiki/Norman_Conquest_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Norman Conquest of England">Norman Conquest</a>, where he was appointed sheriff around 1069. His castle in the town of <a href="/wiki/Worcester" title="Worcester">Worcester</a> encroached on the cathedral cemetery there, angering the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_York" title="Archbishop of York">Archbishop of York</a>. He helped to put down a rebellion against King William I in 1075, and quarrelled with the Church in his county over the jurisdiction of the sheriffs. He continued in the service of William's sons after the king's death, and was appointed <a href="/wiki/Constable" title="Constable">constable</a> under William II and <a href="/wiki/Marshal" title="Marshal">marshal</a> under Henry I. He earned a reputation for extortion, and during the reign of William II, he was considered second only to the king's minister <a href="/wiki/Ranulf_Flambard" title="Ranulf Flambard">Ranulf Flambard</a> in his greediness. Through his daughter, Urse is an ancestor of the Beauchamp family, who eventually became <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Warwick" title="Earl of Warwick">Earls of Warwick</a>. (<a href="/wiki/Urse_d%27Abetot" title="Urse d'Abetot"><b>Full&#160;article...</b></a>)</p>

<h2 id="mp-otd-h2" style="clear:both; margin:0.5em; background:#cedff2; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3b0bf; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span class="mw-headline" id="On_this_day...">On this day...</span></h2>
<div id="mp-otd" style="padding:0.1em 0.6em 0.5em;">
<p><b><a href="/wiki/April_14" title="April 14">April 14</a></b>: <b><a href="/wiki/Pahela_Baishakh" title="Pahela Baishakh">Bengali New Year</a></b>, <b><a href="/wiki/Vaisakhi" title="Vaisakhi">Vaisakhi</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Sikhism" title="Sikhism">Sikhism</a>), <b><a href="/wiki/Puthandu" title="Puthandu">Tamil New Year</a></b>, and <a href="/wiki/April_14#Holidays_and_observances" title="April 14">other New Year festivals</a> in Asia (2018); <b><a href="/wiki/1978_Georgian_demonstrations" title="1978 Georgian demonstrations">Day of the Georgian language</a></b> in Georgia (<a href="/wiki/1978" title="1978">1978</a>)</p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 120px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nuremberg_Apr_14_1561.jpg" class="image" title="Illustration of celestial phenomena in Nuremberg"><img alt="Illustration of celestial phenomena in Nuremberg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Nuremberg_Apr_14_1561.jpg/120px-Nuremberg_Apr_14_1561.jpg" width="120" height="84" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Nuremberg_Apr_14_1561.jpg/180px-Nuremberg_Apr_14_1561.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Nuremberg_Apr_14_1561.jpg/240px-Nuremberg_Apr_14_1561.jpg 2x" data-file-width="340" data-file-height="238" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Illustration of celestial phenomena in Nuremberg</div>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1561" title="1561">1561</a> – In <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, there was a <b><a href="/wiki/1561_celestial_phenomenon_over_Nuremberg" title="1561 celestial phenomenon over Nuremberg">mass sighting of celestial phenomena</a></b> <i>(illustration pictured)</i> where observers described an "aerial battle" between odd-shaped objects.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1908" title="1908">1908</a> – The first <b><a href="/wiki/Hauser_Dam" title="Hauser Dam">Hauser Dam</a></b> in the U.S. state of <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a> failed and caused severe flooding and damage downstream.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1945" title="1945">1945</a> – The <a href="/wiki/4th_Canadian_(Armoured)_Division" class="mw-redirect" title="4th Canadian (Armoured) Division">4th Canadian (Armoured) Division</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Razing_of_Friesoythe" title="Razing of Friesoythe">deliberately destroyed</a></b> the German town of <a href="/wiki/Friesoythe" title="Friesoythe">Friesoythe</a> on the orders of <a href="/wiki/Major_general" title="Major general">Major General</a> <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Vokes" title="Christopher Vokes">Christopher Vokes</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2014" title="2014">2014</a> – <a href="/wiki/Boko_Haram" title="Boko Haram">Boko Haram</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Chibok_schoolgirls_kidnapping" title="Chibok schoolgirls kidnapping">kidnapped</a></b> 276 female students from the Government Secondary School in the town of <a href="/wiki/Chibok" title="Chibok">Chibok</a> in <a href="/wiki/Borno_State" title="Borno State">Borno State</a>, Nigeria.</li>
</ul>

<td style="padding:0 6px 0 0">
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Old_Woman_Cooking_Eggs" class="mw-redirect" title="Old Woman Cooking Eggs">Old Woman Cooking Eggs</a></b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Genre_painting" title="Genre painting">genre painting</a> by <a href="/wiki/Diego_Vel%C3%A1zquez" title="Diego Velázquez">Diego Velázquez</a>. Its date of production is not definitively known, but is thought to be have been completed around 1618, before his definitive move from <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> to <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> in 1623. Velázquez frequently used working-class characters in early paintings like this one, in many cases using his family as models; the old woman here also appears in his <i><a href="/wiki/Christ_in_the_House_of_Martha_and_Mary_(Vel%C3%A1zquez)" title="Christ in the House of Martha and Mary (Velázquez)">Christ in the House of Martha and Mary</a></i> (1618). The painting is in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_National_Gallery" title="Scottish National Gallery">National Gallery of Scotland</a> in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>.</p>
<p><small>Painting: <a href="/wiki/Diego_Vel%C3%A1zquez" title="Diego Velázquez">Diego Velázquez</a></small></p>
